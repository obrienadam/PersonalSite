Research Interests

His research interests are centered around computational fluid dynamics (CFD) of multi-phase flows around particle clusters modelled using the immersed boundary (IB) method, working under the supervision of Professor Markus Bussmann at the University of Toronto Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering (MIE). He specializes in developing parallel CFD software and open source software. In particular, he is interested in applications related to interfacial flows in the presence of particles, which are commonly encountered in industries such as oil and gas, pulp and paper, pharmaceuticals and food. The primary focus of his research is developing robust numerical software for solving incompressible multiphase flows, with complex geometries modelled using the immersed boundary (IB) method rather than traditional unstructured meshes.
