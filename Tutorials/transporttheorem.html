Transport Theorem

The general transport theorem describes the transport of an arbitrary quantity, denoted here as $\phi$. This equation takes the form: \begin{equation} \frac{\partial \rho \phi}{\partial t} + \vec{\nabla} \cdot \rho \vec{u} \phi = \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{\nabla} \Gamma \phi + S_\phi \label{eq:transportTheorem} \end{equation} Eq. \eqref{eq:transportTheorem} describes the transport of a generic quantity $\phi$ by advection, diffusion and generation. Most transport equations in nature can be expressed as a special case of the general transport theorem. For example, the momentum equation of fluid dynamics may be expressed as Eq. \eqref{eq:momentum} \begin{equation} \frac{\partial \rho \vec{u}}{\partial t} + \vec{\nabla} \cdot \rho \vec{u} \otimes \vec{u} = \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{\nabla} \mu \vec{u} - \vec{\nabla} \cdot \vec{\vec{I}} p \label{eq:momentum} \end{equation}
